(the residue) + free L-parameters for every reading.**

## H2 — Free L-parameters come in TWO kinds: selection-σ and height-h

Not all free parameters are choices.  Conjecture: free L-parameters factor into
- **selection-σ** — a *section* of a fibration of readings: *which element* (choice σ, the ultrafilter, the
  p-adic base).  Geometry: a section.
- **height-h** — a *position on a tower*: *how far the iterated distinguishing goes* (the resolution dial /
  modulus, finding (v); the ordinal-tower height; large-cardinal strength).  Geometry: a level.

Both free (no exterior dialer, §5.1), different geometry.  Note the map onto `B`: B's **binary value**
carries a *selection*-σ (the ±1 choice), B's **q=+1 pole** carries a *height* (the modulus / reached-by-none
rate, finding (v)).  So the two parameter-kinds are the two aspects of `B`.

## H3 — ★ The two kinds = the two axes of set-theoretic independence

Set theory has exactly two independence mechanisms: **forcing** (adjoin a generic *filter/selection*) and
**large cardinals** (assert more *height/strength*).  Conjecture: these are *precisely* the two free
L-parameter kinds —
- **forcing = adjoining a free selection-σ** (a generic σ; `axiom_of_choice.md`'s independence prediction,
  Agent B's thread);
- **large cardinals = raising the free height-h** (a taller ordinal tower; not a selection — this is why
  Agent A should find large cardinals are a *different KIND* of parameter than choice, not a wall but not a
  selection-σ either).

If H3 holds, the "five calibrated boundaries" reclassify cleanly: ultrafilter/choice/base = selection-σ
(forcing-side); large cardinals/seminorm-completeness = height-h (large-cardinal/completion-side); and the
