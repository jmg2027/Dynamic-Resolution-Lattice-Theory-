## Where 0 emerges

### Source 1: Signed fold cancellation
`Raw.fold (1, -1, +) r` for a balanced Raw (where #atom_a = #atom_b)
gives `Int 0`.  Here 0 = result of cancellation in ‚Ñ§, not a count
of anything.

### Source 2: Orthogonal-axis quotient
`‚Ñï √ó ‚Ñï / diag` ‚Äî the diagonal `(k, k) : ‚Ñï¬≤` collapses to a single
‚Ñ§-point, which we call `0`.  **0 is the diagonal-collapse artifact.**

### Source 3: Group identity
When ‚Ñ§ becomes a group `(‚Ñ§, +)`, it must have an identity element.
That element is forced to be 0 by the group axioms.  Again, 0
appears as a structural necessity, not derived from atoms.
