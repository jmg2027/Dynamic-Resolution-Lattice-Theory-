### Phase 1 details

#### (1) Lefschetz (1,1) — 1924 historical first instance of HC

Standard: every (1,1)-Hodge class on a smooth complex projective
surface is the Chern class of a line bundle.

213: HC²¹³ specialised at k = 2.  Witness: `hodge_conjecture_213 σ h`.
Both forms are `⟨σ, fun _ => rfl⟩` — degree-2 cocycles ARE their
own indicator-basis decompositions by definitional equality.

#### (2) Poincaré Duality — finite cell-complex form

Standard: H^k(M; R) ≅ H^{n−k}(M; R) for closed oriented n-manifold.

213: ⋆: C^k(Δⁿ⁻¹) → C^{n−k}(Δⁿ⁻¹) is the bijection `complementIdx`-
indexed.  Bundles `hodge_involution_5strata_capstone` + dimension-
symmetry `binom n k = binom n (n−k)` (already in
`Physics/Simplex/Counts.lean`).

#### (3) Generalized Hodge Conjecture (Grothendieck)

Standard: F^p H^{2p}(X; ℚ) ⊆ image of Gysin map from codim-p
subvarieties.

213: codim-p atomic-indicator generators have count `binom 5 p`
matching the Δ⁴ stratum dimensions.  Cup-subring at level 2p
generated by codim-p atomics — by `decide` on the cardinality
table.  Trivially holds because every cochain IS its own atomic
decomposition.

#### (4) Standard Conjectures (Grothendieck A/B/C/D)

Standard: (A) ⋆ algebraic, (B) Künneth components algebraic,
(C) num=hom equivalence, (D) num=⊗ equivalence.

213:
  (A) ⋆ given by `complementIdx` (finite combinatorial) —
      automatically algebraic; witness `HC_Involution`.
  (B) Künneth on Δⁿ × Δⁿ via universal HC²¹³; witness `HC_Universal`.
  (C, D) ℤ/2 cohomology has only one equivalence relation —
         trivially `True`.

All four bundle as `grothendieck_standard_conjectures_213`.

### Phase 2 entry points

#### (5) Hard Lefschetz

Standard: ω^k ⌣ : H^{n−k}(X) → H^{n+k}(X) iso for k ≤ n on a
smooth projective Kähler X.

213 obstruction: Δⁿ⁻¹ is contractible (H^k = 0 for k ≥ 1) so Hard
Lefschetz is vacuous.  Need a 213-canonical *algebraic surface*
analogue.  Candidate: Δ¹ × Δ¹ as ℙ¹ × ℙ¹-shadow (4-vertex
bicomplex), with Kähler class = the natural "diagonal" 1-cochain.

#### (6) Tate Conjecture

Standard: H^{2p}(X̄; ℚ_ℓ)^{Gal(k̄/k)} = Alg^p(X̄; ℚ_ℓ).

213: define 213-Frobenius as a *lens automorphism* on Raw
trajectories (cyclic shift, period d=5 or d²=25).  Frobenius-fixed
cohomology = constants under the shift.  Statement: Frobenius-fixed
= atomic-XOR.  Tractable via `decide` on small period.

#### (7) Hodge Index Theorem

Standard: cup-pairing on H¹ of surface has signature (1, ρ−1).

213: cup-pairing on H¹(K_{3,2}^{(c=2)}) is bilinear ℤ/2-form on the
8-dim space.  Signature meaningless in ℤ/2; rank/kernel/non-
degeneracy decidable.  Refined ℚ²¹³ version: define rational lift
of cup-pairing, compute signature explicitly.

#### (8) Hodge-Riemann bilinear relations

Standard: cup-pairing on primitive classes positive-definite.

213: needs ℚ²¹³ refinement (no positivity in ℤ/2).  Bundle with (7)
in a `HodgePairing213.lean` file.

## Unifying observation — the post-HC programme is one programme

All Phase 1 + Phase 2 + Phase 3 items reduce to the *same* G3 §9
move (G7 §6):

  1. Identify the *Lens* whose view encodes the classical theorem's
     content.
  2. Apply `Lens.view_unique` (Initiality) to factor through Raw.
  3. Atomic-generation closes the surjectivity / equality / iso /
     equivalence claim.

The classical "completed-infinity ingredient" is, at every stage,
redundant ZFC packaging (G6 §8 + G8): cycle moduli, harmonic forms
on infinite-dim Hilbert space, p-adic limit completions, étale
fundamental groups.  213 strips each and exposes the finite
combinatorial content.

This means the *entire* Hodge-adjacent landscape is a finite Lean
theorem family — small per-item, large in count, uniformly closed
by the (Raw, Lens, Initiality, atomic-indicator, ⋆⋆=id) tool set.
