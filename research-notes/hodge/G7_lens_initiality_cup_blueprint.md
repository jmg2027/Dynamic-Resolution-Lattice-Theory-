## 1. The cup-Lens

The cup-product Alexander–Whitney operation
    ⌣ : C^k × C^l → C^{k+l}   (`Math/Cohomology/Cup/Core.cup`)
gives C*(X) the structure of a graded ℤ/2-algebra (`Cup/Ring.lean`,
`Cup/Leibniz.lean`).

Define the **cup-Lens**:

  α := the graded direct sum  ⊕_k Cochain n k

  base_a := the multiplicative unit ε ∈ C⁰  (`Cup.Ring.unit_5` at d=5)
  base_b := the canonical "edge generator" — the indicator of the
            colex-first 1-subset (`Hodge.Involution.v0_5` at d=5)
  combine := cup product, ℤ/2-graded-commutative

Raw → α is determined by these data via the catamorphism `Raw.fold`
(`Firmware/Raw.lean`, `Hypervisor/Lens.lean`).

## 2. Lens initiality applied

By `Lens.view_unique` (`Hypervisor/Lens/Initiality.lean`):

> If `f : Raw → α` satisfies `f a = base_a`, `f b = base_b`, and
> `f (slash x y h) = combine (f x) (f y)`, then `f = view`.

Apply this to `f := image-of-Raw-into-cup-subring(C*)`.  By
construction `f` agrees on base_a (= unit ε), base_b (= v0), and
on slash (= cup).  So **`f = cupLens.view`** uniquely.

The image of `f` is — by the L1/L2/HC²¹³ atomic-generation
principle — *all* of C*(X), since every Cochain is by definitional
equality a XOR-combination of indicator cochains, and indicator
cochains are cup-products of base_a, base_b at each stratum.

Therefore `cupLens.view : Raw → α` is **surjective onto C***, and
descends modulo `B*` to a surjection onto H*.

## 3. HC²¹³ uniform proof from §2

**HC²¹³ statement** (G6 §4): the cup-subring of H* generated by
atomic indicators equals the full Hodge subspace H*.

**Proof sketch.**
  1. By §2, the cup-Lens `cupLens.view` is surjective onto C*.
  2. The cup-subring is the image modulo B* — surjective onto H*.
  3. The Hodge subspace = entire H* (in ℤ/2 the ⋆-eigenspace
     decomposition has only eigenvalue 1, by `⋆⋆ = id` capstone).
  4. Therefore cup-subring = H* = Hodge subspace.  ∎

This bypasses per-complex enumeration: the proof works **uniformly**
for every 213-canonical complex X, regardless of size.
