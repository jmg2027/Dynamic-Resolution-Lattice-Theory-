## Why this matters

DRLT context:
  · G120 closed the universe-constant framing of `N_U` by demoting
    it to `configCount 2`, opening the *level* `n` as a parametric
    family axis.
  · The remaining unparametrised axis is the *base* `d` (currently
    hard-coded to 5 inside `configCount n := 5^(5^n)`).
  · Per `Theory/Atomicity/Five.lean` `atomic_iff_five`, the base
    `d = 5` is **structurally forced** at the physics-lens layer
    (no exterior dialer).  The mathematical family
    `configCountD d n := d^(d^n)` is nonetheless a clean,
    well-typed object for any `d : Nat`; documenting it makes the
    "physics selects `d = 5`" statement crisper and gives later
    work (cross-base modular invariants, dyadic comparisons) a
    canonical home.

Mathematical context:
  · The family `F(d, n) := d^(d^n)` is a depth-2 exponential
    slice of Knuth's up-arrow.  It hits tetration `d↑↑3` exactly
    on the diagonal `d = n`; everywhere else it is the count of
    `n`-variable `d`-valued truth tables (functions `[d]^n → [d]`).
  · The clean recursion `F(d, n+1) = F(d, n)^d` is `Nat.pow_mul`-
    trivial in Lean and immediately gives super-exponential
    growth in `n`.
  · For prime `p` coprime to `d`, the sequence `n ↦ F(d, n) mod p`
    is eventually periodic with period dividing
    `ord_{p-1}(d)` — entry-point for the G119 modular-arithmetic
    infrastructure (Bezout, FLT, F_{p²}, Frobenius).

## Risks + mitigations

  · **`Nat.pow_le_pow_left/_right` may not be 213-native**.
    Mitigation: prove inline before importing; verify
    `lean/E213/Lib/Math/Nat/Pow.lean` first.
  · **`decide` may not close `configCountD 7 2 = 7^49`** (large
    numeral).  Mitigation: state as `configCountD 7 2 = 7^49 := rfl`
    instead — kernel reduction handles `7^49` symbolically.
  · **Universe-constant rhetoric leakage**.  Adding a 2-parameter
    family invites "d is THE substrate base, n is THE level"
    rephrasings, both of which are the failure mode.  Mitigation:
    Phase 7 docstring discipline — every touched docstring
    explicitly cites `Theory.Atomicity.Five` for the physics
    selection, and never claims `d = 5` is "the right choice" in
    a mathematical sense.
  · **Phase 5 modular reduction may surface a hidden Mathlib
    dependency** through `Nat.gcd` or `Nat.Prime` formulations.
    Mitigation: route through 213-native definitions in
    `Lib/Math/ModArith/`; the G119 marathon proved this is
    feasible.

## Open questions

  1. **Is the recursion `configCountD d (n+1) = (configCountD d n)^d`
     `rfl`-provable, or does it require `Nat.pow_mul`?**
     Empirical check during Phase 2 — both endpoints are
     `d^(d^(n+1))` and `(d^(d^n))^d`; both reduce to
     `d^(d^n · d)` via `Nat.pow_succ` and `Nat.pow_mul`.  Should
     be `rfl` if Lean's `Nat.pow_succ` is `def`-equal in the
     right direction; otherwise 2-3 line `rw`.
  2. **Should `numVD d L := d^L` be added alongside `numV`, or
     should `numV` migrate to a binary signature?**
     Recommendation from Lean-roadmap audit: add alongside (15
     call sites in numV consumers; binary-signature migration is
     pure refactor pain with no gain).
  3. **Does the `Theory.Atomicity.Five` chain need an explicit
     "d-generalisation forbidden at physics lens" capstone**, or
     is the existing `atomic_iff_five` sufficient?
     Open.  A small wrapper theorem
     `physics_lens_d_eq_five : Atomicity.Atomic d → d = 5` would
     make the gating explicit at the cost of one more theorem.
  4. **Does the "Fibonacci low-order" coincidence
     `(NS, NT, d) = (F₄, F₃, F₅)`** have an independent forcing
     argument, or is it downstream of the atomicity chain?
     Per the structural-origin audit: currently downstream (no
     independent forcing).  An explicit Lean theorem
     `fibonacci_atomic_downstream : ¬ ∃ alternative atomic shape
     compatible with (Fₖ₋₁, Fₖ₋₂)` would seal the question.
  5. **Modular periodicity at `(d, p) = (5, 7)` — exact period?**
     Computational answer is 2 (per number-theoretic survey);
     Phase 5 proof would consume `UniversalFLT.FLTMain` +
     order computation.
  6. **Diagonal limit**.  `configCountD d d = d↑↑3`.  Is there a
     DRLT-internal use for the diagonal sequence
     `d ↦ configCountD d d`?  Currently no — open.
  7. **Truth-table reading**.  `configCountD d n = | [d]^n → [d] |`.
     Is this combinatorial identity worth a Lean theorem via
     `Fin (d^n) → Fin d` cardinality, or is it `rfl` at the type
     level?  Open; if `Fin.fintype` is 213-native this is
     trivial.
  8. **Pisano-style period table**.  Build a `decide`-checked
     table of `configCountD 5 n mod p` for `p ∈ {2, 3, 7, 11, 13,
     17, 19, 23, 29, 31}` and `n ∈ {0, ..., 10}`?  Useful for
     numerical audit; cheap.
  9. **Gram self-energy structural derivation**.  The ppb-level
     `1/α_em` closure currently uses `213 / 10⁸ = α_em² / d²` with
     `α_em` seeded from observation
     (`AlphaEM/Augmented.lean:134-141`).  Is there a Lens reading
     that derives this term from atomic primitives alone?  Closing
     this removes the "one observation-seeded coefficient" caveat
     from the precision-Lens validation standard.  Out of scope
     for G123 (lives in the physics layer), logged here as the
     principal downstream physics open problem.
 10. **C2a / C2b / C2c triple-constraint parametric closure**.
     `AtomicConstantsUnique.lean` proves `(m, n, c, d) = (3, 2, 2, 5)`
     is unique up to bound 300 (bounded search + closed-form
     Diophantine for `n ≥ 4` having no solution).  A fully
     parametric ∀-(m, n) ∅-axiom proof of C2b uniqueness would
     seal "(3, 2, 2, 5) is the unique solution" without the
     bound qualifier.  Mentioned in the structural-origin audit
     punchlist; not on G123 critical path but a clean follow-up.
 11. **Catalogue forcing vs catalogue fitting**.
     `rust-engine/docs/closure-algorithm.md` claims 9 distinct

     d, c} = {3, 2, 5, 2}.  An independent verification —

     the fraction that fit — would quantify "forcing" vs
