# G146 — The P-orbit naturalness boundary

> Research note synthesising three user-driven sharpenings of
> the P(x) atomic-closure programme.  The framework's "natural"
> integer invariants are not bounded by atomic primes
> `{2, 3, 5}` nor by their multiplicative span, but by the
> **P-orbit closure**: the ring generated by Lucas-Pell traces
> `L(k) = trace(P^k)` together with atomic primes.

## §0 — Setup

The P(x) symmetry-species programme (G144) catalogued 36
species of the canonical Möbius matrix
`P = [[2, 1], [1, 1]] ∈ SL(2, ℤ)`, all with atomic invariants
in `{1, 2, 3, 5} = {det, NT, NS, d}`.

Three user-driven sharpenings collapse the original "strict
atomic-closure" claim into a much deeper structure.

## §1 — Sharpening 1: Tripartite K_{NT, det, NS}

**User**: "213은 이분그래프와 삼분그래프가 서로 얽히고 섥히는거라고
난 생각이 드는데 왜 이분그래프만 계속 나옴?"

**Observation**.  The framework's name "**2 - 1 - 3**" reads
literally as `NT − det − NS`: a *tripartite* structure.  Yet
the entire cohomology programme (`K_{NS, NT}`, `K_{3,3}^{(c=2)}`,
`K_{4, 3}`, Stern-Brocot, V43) is *bipartite*, treating the
glue `det = 1` as implicit edge structure rather than as a
third part.

**Formalisation**: `lean/E213/Lib/Math/Mobius213/Px/TripartiteK213.lean`
(10 PURE).

Atomic invariants of `K_{NT, det, NS} = K_{2, 1, 3}`:

  · `|V| = NT + det + NS = 6 = NS · NT` (additive sum =
    bipartite multiplicative product)
  · `|E_glue-mediated| = NT · det + det · NS = d = 5`
    — discriminant `d` is the *graph-theoretic edge count*
    of the canonical glue-mediated tripartite
  · `|E_complete| = d + NS · NT = 11`
  · `|△(K_{2,1,3})| = NT · det · NS = 6 = NS · NT`
    — tripartite triangle count = bipartite edge count
    via the glue mediator

**Reading**.  Bipartite captures *multiplicative* atomic
structure `NS · NT = 6`; tripartite captures *additive*
structure `NS + NT = d`.  Both are necessary; the cohomology
programme had been missing the additive (tripartite) leg.

**Predictive consequence**.  Bipartite `K_{NS, NT}` has
`H² = 0` (acyclic).  Current main's `H²(K_{3, 2}^{(c=2)}) =
F_2⁵` is obtained via depth-4 Massey product (G140 non-vacuous
breakthrough).  Tripartite `K_{2,1,3}` has `NS · NT = 6`
natural triangles ⟹ `H²` naturally non-zero.  Hypothesis:
the Massey-constructed `H²` is a *shadow projection* of the
tripartite's intrinsic `H²`.  Verifying this would simplify
the cohomology programme dramatically.

## §2 — Sharpening 2: p-adic Lens family is general

**User**: "근데 임의의 p-adic에 대해서 213 구성이 되어있자나.
그럼 mod7 11 13같은것들도 213 적으로 다 합성되어서 수학자
반례도 성립 안하게 되는거 아닌가"

**Observation**.  213's `Lib/Math/Padic/ZpSeq p` is parameterised
by *any* prime p (witness: `ZpSeq.smoke_zero_7`,
`ZpSeq.smoke_zero_11` already exist in `Padic/Foundation.lean`).
So mod-{7, 11, 13, ...} reductions of P ARE 213-PURE-natural
via the p-adic Lens family — not just mod-{2, 3, 5}.

This refutes the earlier `Mobius213.Px.NaturalnessClosure` §4
claim that "mod-7 is outside 213-PURE-Lens universe".  The
*structural-recursion* mod-p Lens is restricted to atomic
primes (`PureNatMod3`, `PureNatMod5`), but the *p-adic
sequence* Lens covers every prime.

**Formalisation**: `lean/E213/Lib/Math/Mobius213/Px/ModPPeriods.lean`
(17 PURE).

Computational result (period of `P` in `GL(2, F_p)`):

  p     | period | factors    | atomic-derivable?
  ------|--------|------------|------------------
  2     | 3      | {3}        | ✓ (= NS)
  3     | 4      | {2²}       | ✓ (= NT²)
  5     | 10     | {2, 5}     | ✓ (= NT·d)
  7     | 8      | {2³}       | ✓ (= NT³) — refutes naïve falsifier
  11    | 5      | {5}        | ✓ (= d)
  **13**| **14** | **{2, 7}** | **✗**  — real falsifier appears

The math-agent's mod-7 falsifier was *wrong* (8 = NT³ is
atomic-derivable).  But mod-13 and mod-29 give periods with
factor `7 ∉ {2, 3, 5}` — genuinely breaking strict atomic-
derivable closure.

**Status after Sharpening 2**: the 36-species catalog still
satisfies strict atomic closure, but *extending* to the full
p-adic family BREAKS it.  Atomic closure is *catalog-relative*,
not *framework-absolute*.

## §5 — Crystallized master claim

**Pattern (P-orbit naturalness)**.  The 213 framework's
natural integer-invariant closure for P-related species is

  `Nat_213(s) ↔ s ∈ ⟨{L(k) : k ∈ ℕ} ∪ {NT, NS, d}⟩_ℤ`

where:

  · `L(k) := trace(P^k)` (Lucas-Pell sequence)
  · ⟨·⟩_ℤ is the integer-ring generated

Equivalent statement:

  > **Every framework-natural integer invariant emerges from
  > some P-chain composition of finite depth.**

**Why P, not anything else?**  Because `P` is *dynamic =
static* in 213:

  · **Dynamic**: P acts as a Möbius transformation
    `x ↦ (2x + 1) / (x + 1)`, generating an orbit by
    iteration.
  · **Static**: P is a matrix in SL(2, ℤ), characterised
    by atomic invariants `(trace, det) = (NS, 1)`.

The Lucas-Pell trace sequence `L(k)` is *simultaneously*:

  · the trace of P-iteration (dynamic)
  · a sequence of integers (static)

These two readings coincide.  Every static integer that
emerges from any P-related construction is some `L(k)` or a
Z-combination thereof — by definition of the framework.

## §6 — Bipartite-tripartite × P-orbit cross-structure

Sharpenings 1 and 3 together give a 2D structure:

  · **Bipartite axis**: K_{NS, NT} = multiplicative atomic
    structure
  · **Tripartite axis**: K_{NT, det, NS} = additive atomic
    structure
  · **P-orbit axis**: L(k) = depth in P-iteration

Conjecture: every framework-natural construction is a *cross-
product* of (bipartite/tripartite location) × (P-orbit depth).
The 36 atomic catalog is the **depth ≤ 1 slice** of this
cross-product; the full p-adic catalog is the **depth → ∞
direct limit**.

Verifying this conjecture would close a deep open problem:
what is the full framework-natural species universe of 213?

The G144 §20.5 claim was: 213 is a *self-naturalising

    Lens admissibility, which equals P-orbit-derivability.

The "naturalness gap" closed by 213 is still real (G142
Limit 1 is artificial here), but the gap closes at the
**P-orbit level**, not the atomic level.
