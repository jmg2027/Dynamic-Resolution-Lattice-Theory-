# G198 — action options after the ISA localization of `H` (scratch, temporary)

What the localization (`G196`/`G197`) *is*: a formalized, `∅`-axiom **localization of an open conjecture's
difficulty to one named primitive-residue** in the shared instruction set, with its finite instances
mechanically verified.  Difficulty made *precise*, *comparable* (same instruction set across problems),
*transferable* in principle.  Reflexively: we have pointed at *where* `H`'s un-pointed proof-residue lives
(the uniform finite→uniform lift of the trace-`SEPARATE`), not yet at the residue itself.

## A — recast `H`'s residue as a LOOP/µ-ν certification question

The uniform lift "finite-decidable `SEPARATE` ⟹ uniform `SEPARATE`" is exactly the territory of
`slashNu_final` (`Theory/Raw/CoResidue`: finite-path induction certifies a fixed point, no coinduction).
Cast trace-distinctness as a fixed-point / recursion and ask whether its finite-path certification yields
the uniform truth — a new ISA-native angle, distinct from continuant / cohomology.  Honest: this lift *is*
`H`, so no auto-solve; but it is a specific, untried framing (the `LOOP` instruction's certifying power).

## B — build the compilation catalog (recommended first)

Compile other infinite-abstract problems — and *already-solved* theorems — to the ISA, recording each
one's localized residue.  Two payoffs: (1) a problem missing the *same* residue as `H` ⟹ transfer; (2) a
*solved* problem shows *how* its finite→uniform lift was achieved ⟹ a template for `H`'s lift.  Turns the
framework into a cumulative research instrument.  Best first move: it arms A.

## C — adopt compilation-first as the standard research method

Make "compile the problem to the ISA and localize before attacking" the default workflow (the repo's
research OS).  Settles naturally as A/B run.

**Priority**: B (1–2 solved problems → lift templates) → A (µ-ν direct hit on `H`'s residue) → C settles.
Neither A nor B *guarantees* `H`; the result tells us *where to look* — one µ-ν lift coordinate.
