## The decomposition (C / Reading / Residue)

- **Construction `C`** — a **tower of nested distinguishings on the derivative axis**. Where
  `galois_correspondence.md`'s `C` adjoins *roots of a polynomial* (algebraic distinguishings),
  differential Galois adjoins *solutions of a linear ODE and their derivatives* — a **differential**
  distinguishing-history. A Picard–Vessiot extension `K⟨y₁,…,yₙ⟩/K` is the smallest differential
  field generated by a fundamental solution set: the bracket-chain / dyadic point-construction of
  `derivative.md`/`integration.md` carrying a value-construction at each, now closed under the
  derivation `d`. "Solvable by quadratures" = the tower is built by **adjoining integrals
  (`∫`, the resolution-reading's accumulation, `L_Σ` of `integration.md`)** and exponentials of
  integrals — the resolution-axis analogue of "adjoin an n-th root".

- **Reading `L`** — **two readings, one inherited verbatim from `galois.md`, one swapped in.**
  1. *The Aut-family reading (unchanged from `groups.md`/`galois_correspondence.md`):* the
     **differential Galois group** `Gal_∂(K⟨y⟩/K)` = the `K`-and-`d`-preserving self-readings (the
     automorphisms commuting with the derivation and fixing `K`). This is *exactly* `groups.md`'s
     composition-closed Aut-family — the only difference from ordinary Galois is the word
     "commuting with `d`" in the preservation clause. The Kolchin correspondence is then
     `galois_correspondence.md`'s `Fix ⊣ Inv` closure (`clo = Inv∘Fix`, `q=+1` idempotent) on the
     lattice of **intermediate differential subfields** ↔ Zariski-closed subgroups.
  2. *The swapped reading:* the **`∫`/resolution reading `L_Σ`** of `integration.md` replaces
     `galois.md`'s radical reading. "Adjoin a radical `ⁿ√a`" becomes "adjoin a **quadrature**
     `∫f` (or `exp∫f`)" — the resolution dial's accumulation reading at residue resolution.

- **Residue (`q=±1`)** — read **twice**, at the two poles, exactly as `galois_correspondence.md`:
  - the **closure residue** (`clo`, `q=+1`) governs the **Kolchin correspondence** — the bijection
    lives on the closed differential subfields / closed subgroups (residue collapses to closure,
    `clo_idempotent`); identical in shape to `galois_correspondence.md`'s FT and
    `convex_duality.md`'s `f**=clo`;
  - the **commutator/derived-series residue** (`q=±1`) governs **solvability by quadratures**: the
    derived series of `Gal_∂` terminates (`q=+1` converge = solvable-by-quadratures) or is perfect
    and never descends (`q=−1` escape = no quadrature tower resolves the ODE), the *same*
    `isSolvable`/`isPerfect` tag as `Solvable.lean`.
  - **Liouville's theorem is a *third* face of the residue** — the `q=−1` escape on `L_Σ` itself,
    one resolution layer below the group: the antiderivative reading `∫e^{-x²}` forces a value
    **outside the elementary-function differential field** — the surplus `L_Σ` forces but cannot
    capture (`integration.md`'s "+C" residue sharpened to "no elementary `F` with `F'=f`"). The
    same fixed-point-free escape `object1_not_surjective`/`no_surjection_of_fixedpointfree` drives:
    no elementary `F` is fixed by the `∫`-reading's self-application on `e^{-x²}`.
