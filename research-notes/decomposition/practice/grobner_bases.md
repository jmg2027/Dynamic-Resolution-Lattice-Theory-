## The decomposition (C / Reading / Residue)

- **Construction `C`** — distinguishing, iterated, on **polynomials**. A monomial `x₁^{a₁}…xₙ^{aₙ}` is a
  count-tuple over the variable axes — the **vector ×-count reading** of `prime_factorization.md`
  (variables = distinguishable ×-atoms, exponents = per-axis counts; the same atom-distinguishability that
  makes `vp` a free ℕ-module readout, `Meta/Nat/VpMul.lean:165 vp_mul`). A polynomial is a finite
  weighted sum of such monomials (the **weight axis**, `probability.md`/`measure.md`: a value-weighted
  count of monomials). An ideal `I = ⟨g₁,…,gₘ⟩` is the sub-construction generated by the `gᵢ` under
  `+` and ×-by-monomial — the *moves* of a rewriting system. So `C` here is exactly the polynomial-ideal
  point of `algebraic_geometry.md`'s ideal lattice (degree = `C`'s fold-height; a *prime* ideal = the
  ×-atom of that lattice).

- **Reading `L`** — the **reduction / normalization reading** read on polynomials mod a basis, three
  parameters bundled:
  - **the leading-term reading `LT` = a Lens.** A monomial order is a total well-order on monomials;
    `LT(f)` reads off the largest monomial of `f` under it. This is a projection (forget all but the
    head term) — `Lens.view` on a polynomial, the analogue of `OneDiagonal`'s "read the head". The
    **leading-term ideal `LT(I) = ⟨LT(f) : f∈I⟩`** is the Lens applied across the ideal.
  - **the division algorithm = the reduction step / the fold.** Dividing `f` by `(g₁,…,gₘ)`: while some
    `gᵢ`'s leading term divides a term of `f`, subtract the matching multiple — a one-step rewrite
    `f → f − (cx^α/LT(gᵢ))·gᵢ` that strictly lowers the leading monomial. Iterated to a remainder, this
    is **`freeReduce`** on polynomials: a single structural pass that, when the basis is Gröbner, reaches
    a unique normal form. The remainder = `Raw.fold` to the normal form (`SemanticAtom.lean:412
    raw_initial`; the unique arrow out of the free object).
  - **Buchberger's algorithm = the confluence completion.** Add S-polynomials' nonzero remainders to the
    basis until *every* S-polynomial reduces to 0 (Buchberger's criterion) — i.e. until the rewriting is
    **confluent**. This is exactly the FreeReduction recipe: complete the rewriting system so distinct
    reduction paths agree (`free_group_quotient_no_quot`).

- **Residue** — the reduction reading's self-application surplus, at the **q=+1 converging pole only**
  (the load-bearing find — contrast §SPINE):
  - **q=+1 (terminate + confluent + decidable) — the WHOLE corner.** Termination: the monomial order is a
    well-order, so no infinite descending reduction chain exists — `Lambek.no_infinite_descent`
    (`Lambek.lean:273`), the descent bottoms out at the normal form (`terminal_iff_atom`, `:308`), each
    step drops at least the unit of the height measure (`part_depth_succ_le`, `:245` = the leading-monomial
    drop). Confluence: Buchberger's completion makes critical pairs (S-polynomials) join, so the normal
    form is *unique* — the residue **settles** (`free_group_quotient_no_quot`, the `Quot`-free normal
    form). Decidability: `f∈I ⟺ f →* 0` is the decidable word problem (`freeEquiv_iff_reduce_eq`). The
    `q=+1` converging unimodular pole (`ResidueTag.lean:228 residue_tag_two_poles`,
    `:160 converge_residue_fixed`, `:180 golden_is_converge`).

## THE REVELATION — a Gröbner basis IS the colimit Side-A confluent+terminating normal form on polynomial ideals; Buchberger = the confluence completion; reduced GB = the Quot-free canonical rep; membership = the decidable word problem; and (unlike groups) it is FULLY DECIDABLE = the q=+1 corner closed

**COLLAPSE 1 — a Gröbner basis = a confluent+terminating rewriting system for the ideal = the colimit
Side-A.** `SYNTHESIS.md` §5.1 records the program's second wall-style result: a quotient by
move-generated equivalence is "= normal forms" *exactly when* the rewriting is **confluent + terminating**
(Newman + Church–Rosser), and that Side-A is **built ∅-axiom** in `FreeReduction.lean` (26/0) — the free
group as a normal-form Σ-quotient with `proj_val_eq_iff` (`:237`), no `Quot`, no `Classical`, no Mathlib.
The polynomial-ideal instance is *literally that*: the basis `G` defines a one-step reduction
`→_G` lowering the leading monomial; "`G` is a Gröbner basis" is *the same statement* as "`→_G` is
confluent" (Buchberger's criterion), and termination is the well-order on monomials. So a Gröbner basis is
**not a new construction to fear** — it is the decidable `Quot`-free corner the calculus already built,
read on `k[x₁..xₙ]` instead of `FreeWord`. The division algorithm = `freeReduce` (`:141`, a single
structural pass), the remainder = the unique normal form, Buchberger = the completion that earns
confluence. This is the SAME colimit Side-A machinery already named four ways across `free_corner.md` /
`cut_elimination.md` / `homotopy_theory.md` (Ho(C) = the `LensImage`/`FreeReduction` Σ-quotient,
`homotopy_theory.md:119`) / the colimit synthesis — now a *fifth* instance, on ideals.

**COLLAPSE 2 — the reduced Gröbner basis = the `Quot`-free canonical representative
(`free_group_quotient_no_quot`'s analogue).** A reduced Gröbner basis (monic, autoreduced) is the
*unique* canonical basis for an ideal under a fixed monomial order — the textbook "canonical form" of an
ideal. That is exactly `proj_section` (`FreeReduction.lean:242`): the normal-form section is onto, every
class has one canonical representative, and `freeReduce_idempotent` (`:191`) is "the reduced basis is its
own reduction" — applying the autoreduction twice = applying it once. The bundle
`free_group_quotient_no_quot` (`:264`) packages exactly the five facts a reduced Gröbner basis delivers:
(1) the Σ-quotient characterisation `proj_val_eq_iff`, (2) the decidable word problem
`freeEquiv_iff_reduce_eq`, (3) idempotent normalisation `freeReduce_idempotent`, (4) reducedness of every
normal form `freeReduce_reduced`, (5) the section is onto `proj_section`. Term-for-term, that is "reduced
Gröbner basis = the canonical autoreduced confluent system."

**COLLAPSE 3 — ideal membership = the decidable word problem.** `f ∈ I ⟺ NF_G(f) = 0` (the remainder of
dividing `f` by a Gröbner basis `G` is zero) is the canonical decision procedure of computational
commutative algebra. This IS `freeEquiv_iff_reduce_eq` (`:216`): two words are equivalent iff their
reductions agree, so `f ≡ 0 (mod I) ⟺ freeReduce(f) = freeReduce(0) = 0`. **Buchberger's algorithm makes
ideal membership decidable — the SAME decidability the confluent rewriting gives.**

**FORCING — termination is forced by the well-founded monomial order already in `C`.** Strong termination
of the division algorithm is not stipulated; the monomial order is a well-order, so the leading-monomial
strictly drops at every reduction step and no infinite reduction chain exists —
`Lambek.no_infinite_descent` (`:273`), the descent reaching the normal-form floor in `≤ depth+1` steps
(`terminal_iff_atom`, `:308`), each step the irreducible unit drop `part_depth_succ_le` (`:245`). The
height measure `Lambek.isPart_wf` (`:199`) is the abstract content of "monomial order is well-founded".
The S-polynomial = the **critical pair** (the confluence test): the unique minimal overlap of two leading
terms, whose reduction-to-0 is exactly "this critical pair joins" — Newman's lemma (local confluence +
termination ⟹ confluence) is the abstract Buchberger criterion, and termination is the
`no_infinite_descent` floor that lets it apply.
