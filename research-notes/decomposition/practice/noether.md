# Decomposition: Noether's theorem — symmetry → conservation

*213-decomposition of "every symmetry forces a conserved quantity", per `../README.md` (model
v4). Builds directly on `groups.md` (a symmetry = an element/subfamily of `Aut(C)`, the
composition-closed family of `C`-preserving readings) and `determinant.md` (the conserved
unimodular scalar `det₂ = 1`). The leverage hypothesis to **derive**, not just re-see: a symmetry
— an action of `Aut(C)` — **forces** a conserved readout, an `L`-value invariant under that action,
and the calculus *predicts which readout* because "a reading invariant under all of `Aut(C)`" **is**
the definition of a conserved quantity. Certified instance: `det₂ = 1` conserved under the
Stern-Brocot/Markov tree action.*

## The decomposition

- **Construction `C`** — the same distinguishing-structure of `groups.md`/`determinant.md`: a
  ×-construction built by composition. Concretely the **Stern-Brocot / modular matrix tree** — `2×2`
  bundles of directed counts generated by `mul`, with a tree of nodes `mNode path`, `cNode path`
  (`SternBrocotMarkov.lean`, `UnimodularSynthesis.lean`).

- **The Aut-action (the symmetry)** — a subfamily of `Aut(C)`: the **left/right child moves on the
  tree**, i.e. right-multiplication by the det-1 generators `L = [[1,0],[1,1]]`, `R = [[1,1],[0,1]]`
  (`HolonomyLattice.lean` §4), and more generally any composition of `SL₂(ℤ)` nodes. Each move is a
  `C`-preserving self-reading — it relabels the tree without disturbing the distinguishing-history,
  exactly `groups.md`'s `composeList`/`mulPerm` shape one realisation up. The orbit of this action is
  the whole tree (every positive rational reached by a unique `L,R` word).

- **Reading `L` (the candidate conserved readout)** — the **character reading `det₂`** of
  `determinant.md`: `M ↦ M.a·M.d − M.b·M.c`. This is the reading whose homomorphism property
  `det₂(MN) = det₂ M · det₂ N` (`det2_mul`) is *forced* by `det` reading off the ×-construction.

- **Residue** — `q = ±1`, the orientation/multiplier bit (`CassiniUnimodular.det_step`:
  `det s (n+1) = q · det s n`). The conserved case is the `q = +1` pole; the `q = −1` pole is the
  alternating (period-2) residue. Conservation and its failure are *one law at the two residue
  signs* (`cassini_law_one_at_two_multipliers`) — the same `q = ±1` residue tag the README's model
  v4 carries.

## Re-seeing

```
  a symmetry              =  an action of Aut(C)         (right-mult by SL₂ generators on the tree)
  a conserved quantity    =  a reading L invariant under all of Aut(C)
  "symmetry → conservation" =  the character det₂ is fixed by the action  (det₂(M·g)=det₂ M·det₂ g, det₂ g = 1)
  Noether (this instance) =  det₂ = 1 on every node of the orbit          (mNode_det1, unimodular_drives_tree_and_markov)
  the conserved current   =  det₂ propagated by the homomorphism det2_mul along any path
  conservation around a loop = det_holonomy_eq_one  (det = 1 around the whole loop)
  the closed form         =  det s n = qⁿ · det s 0   (det_closed); conserved ⟺ q = +1
```

The Noether shape — *a group action with a scalar invariant under it* — is present, certified, and
∅-axiom. The action is the `SL₂` tree-move; the invariant is `det₂`; invariance is the homomorphism
`det2_mul` evaluated where the generators carry `det = 1`.

## LEVERAGE — does the calculus PREDICT the conserved readout from the symmetry?

This is the load-bearing question, and the answer splits cleanly.

**(1) The certified Noether instance is real (not asserted).** `det₂ = 1` is conserved under the
Stern-Brocot/Markov action, end to end in ∅-axiom Lean:

- the action's generators carry the invariant: `mInterval_det`, `cInterval_det` (`det = 1`);
- the invariant is propagated by the homomorphism along the action: `det2_mul`
  (`det₂(MN) = det₂ M · det₂ N`, one `ring_intZ`), giving `mNode_det1` over the *whole* tree;
- it is conserved around any closed loop: `HolonomyLattice.det_holonomy_eq_one`
  (`∀ w, (∀ g ∈ w, det g = 1) → det (holonomy w) = 1`);
- the **same** invariant simultaneously drives two domains (`unimodular_drives_tree_and_markov`:
  one `det₂ = 1` yields Farey monotonicity *and* the Markov Vieta jump) and is carried by four
  independent constructions (`unimodular_four_readings`). One symmetry, one conserved scalar, many
  readings — the precise Noether picture.

**(2) The calculus PREDICTS *which* readout is conserved — genuine leverage, with one honest
qualifier.** The prediction is not "there exists some invariant" (vacuous); it is *the character
reading is the conserved one*, and it is forced by two facts already in the calculus:

- A reading is invariant under the action **iff** it is a homomorphism on `C`'s composition *and*
  the action's generators sit in its kernel-of-change (here: read to the unit `1`). The character
  reading is by `determinant.md` the *unique* reading forced to satisfy `det₂(MN)=det₂ M·det₂ N`
  (because it reads off the ×-construction `mul`). So among all readings of `C`, the calculus
  *names in advance* the character as the one that can be Aut-invariant — exactly as `entropy.md`'s
  `−log` was named in advance as the unique character turning a product into a sum. **The structure
  "Aut-invariant readout = a character whose action-generators read to the unit" is predictive: it
  tells you to look at the character `det₂`, not at the matrix interior (which the residue forgets).**
- The conserved *value* and the *failure* of conservation are then derived as one parametric law:
  `det_closed` gives `det s n = qⁿ · det s 0`, so conservation ⟺ the action is unimodular at the
  `q = +1` pole. The calculus predicts not just *that* `det₂` is conserved but *the condition under
  which it is* (`q = +1`) and *what replaces conservation when it fails* (alternation at `q = −1`,
  `cassini_law_one_at_two_multipliers`). That conditional structure is more than description.

**The honest qualifier (prediction vs description).** What the calculus genuinely *predicts* is the
**form**: "the conserved readout is the character of `C`, fixed at the unit by the symmetry's
generators." That is a derivation — it picks the readout out of all possible ones and gives the
`q = ±1` conservation/failure dichotomy. What it does **not** do is the analytic Noether *machine*:
classical Noether takes a *continuous* one-parameter symmetry of an action functional and produces,
by varying the Lagrangian, a *specific* conserved current `∂_μ j^μ = 0`. There is **no Lagrangian,
no continuous flow, no `∂_μ j^μ = 0` in the repo**; the action here is the *discrete* `SL₂` tree
move and the "current" is `det₂` propagated by `det2_mul`. So this is Noether's **structural
skeleton** (group action ⊢ invariant scalar) *predicted and certified*, not Noether's **variational
derivation** (continuous-symmetry-of-action ⊢ current). The leverage is real at the skeleton level;
it is a collapse-plus-forcing, short of the full analytic theorem.

**Verdict: PREDICTION (structural), short of the full variational theorem.** The calculus predicts
the conserved readout (the character) from the symmetry (`Aut`-action) and derives the conservation
condition (`q = +1`), certified by `det_holonomy_eq_one` + `det_closed`. It does *not* reconstruct
the continuous-symmetry → conserved-current machine. So: leverage on the "symmetry forces an
Aut-invariant character" law; honest miss on variational Noether proper.

## Note for the technique — does Noether confirm "Aut-invariant readout = conserved quantity" as a derivable law?

**YES, as a structural law; it is the cleanest tie of `groups.md` to physics so far.** `groups.md`
established the *domain* side: a symmetry is `Aut(C)`, the composition-closed reading-family.
`determinant.md` established the *codomain* side: a character is a reading whose readout is a
number-construction. Noether is the statement that **these two compose into an invariance**: a
character reading that is fixed by every element of `Aut(C)` is a conserved quantity, and the
calculus *predicts it must be the character* (the only reading forced to be a homomorphism on `C`'s
composition). This is the same single `×↦·` arrow yet again — `det2_mul` = `vp_mul` = parity `L₂` =
entropy additivity (`entropy.md`) = independence (`probability.md`) — now read as **invariance under
a group action** rather than as a homomorphism into a number. Noether thus adds no new primitive: it
is the **fixed-point set of the character under the `Aut`-action**, and "conserved quantity" is the
name for that fixed-point set. Model v4 holds.

**Tie to physics (genuine, but thin where it matters).** The conserved `det = 1` is not abstract: it
is the **flat discrete connection** of `HolonomyLattice` (`det_holonomy_eq_one`), and
`MetricHolonomyBridge.lean` identifies that holonomy generator `S` with the gravity metric's `J`
(Regge "gravity = deficit angle" = "gravity = metric"). So the conserved unimodular scalar is the
213 stand-in for a flat connection / no-curvature condition — a conservation law with a physics
reading. **But this is honestly thin as Noether-for-physics:** there is no `∂_μ j^μ = 0`, no
energy/momentum/charge current derived from a continuous symmetry; the repo's `MetricHolonomyBridge`
itself flags (its own CAVEAT) that it has only one *flat* `Δ⁴`, no curvature field and no connection
transporting the metric over a glued lattice. The physics conservation that *is* present
(`PhiResidueGlue.residue_unit_three_scales`: the orbit determinant `det Pⁿ`, the φ-residue `W`, and
the atomic glue `NS − NT = 1` are *one conserved unit at three scales*) is again the *same*
unimodular invariant — corroborating "conserved = Aut-invariant character" across three readings,
but still the discrete/structural conservation, not the variational current.

So Noether **confirms** the derivable law *"Aut-invariant character = conserved quantity"* and ties
`groups.md` (the action) to `determinant.md`/physics (the invariant) — a real leverage at the
structural level — while the continuous-symmetry variational theorem remains an open frontier (no
Lagrangian / no `∂_μ j^μ` in the repo).
