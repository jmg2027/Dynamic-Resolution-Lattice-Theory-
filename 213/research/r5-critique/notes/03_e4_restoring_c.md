# 03 — E4: what might restore ℂ-uniqueness?

## Setup

Given E1 (ZI counterexample), E2 (Z2 counterexample), and E3
(R5b is the smuggling channel), the remaining question is:

> **Is there a 213-internal condition that uniquely selects ℂ
> among R1–R4-admissible Lenses?**

If yes, 213 has an authentic internal reason for ℂ's
specialness (with physical implications). If no, ℂ is a
convention and Paper 1's uniqueness theorem rests on
external (classical) axioms.

## Candidate conditions

### (a) Cardinality bound

Any bound ≥ ℵ₀ admits ZI and Z2 (countable).  A bound ≥ `𝔠`
is exactly R5b restated — not 213-internal.

### (b) Archimedean / ordered

ℂ is **not** archimedean (no order compatible with its
algebra).  ℝ is.  "Ordered field" picks ℝ, not ℂ.

### (c) Algebraic closure

ℂ is algebraically closed; ZI and Z2 are not.  But algebraic
closure is a classical property — constructing roots in
general requires non-finitary reasoning.

### (d) Raw-internal limit / growth

"view preserves a Raw-internal `depth`-style ℕ-valued
attribute"?  Any unital commutative ring with 1 admits
`ℕ → α` via `n ↦ n · 1`; ZI and Z2 satisfy this equally.

### (e) Categorical universal property

Is ℂ the initial / terminal object of some category of
R1–R4-admissible Lenses?  Unclear.  Investigation needed.

### (f) Tied to physics

ℂ appears in quantum mechanics because of specific empirical
/geometric reasons (interference, unitarity).  This is NOT an
internal 213 property — it is external evidence that ℂ is
the physically relevant Lens.

If 213's endgame is physics, this is acceptable: ℂ = the
**physical** Lens, not the unique mathematical Lens.  Paper 1's
"ℂ is unique" claim would then be philosophically a category
error: it claims internal necessity where only external
empirical fit exists.

## Provisional conclusion

Of the six candidates, only (e) — a categorical universal
property — would preserve any form of 213-internal
ℂ-uniqueness without smuggling.  Remaining candidates either:
- are equivalent to classical assumptions (a, b, c)
- are satisfied by ZI, Z2, and beyond (d)
- move the claim to external empirical grounding (f)

**Path forward for Paper 2**: adopt (f) framing —
ℂ is the physical Lens, not an internal mathematical
necessity.  This is consistent with the user's earlier
clarification:
*"ℂ가 특이한거고 나머지 렌즈가 수학이야"*.
