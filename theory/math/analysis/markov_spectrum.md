# The Markov spectrum from the golden form — `√5`, `√8`, and the Vieta tree

**Status**: Closed for the first two spectrum values and the tree engine; the full spectrum
is the documented continuation.  Source of truth (all ∅-axiom):
`lean/E213/Lib/Math/NumberSystems/Real213/{GoldenFormMarkov, MarkovTree}` (19 PURE / 0 dirty).

## Overview

The single-number irrationality measure `μ` collapses the whole stratum `{μ = 2}` to one
value.  The **Lagrange/Markov spectrum** refines it: for the `μ = 2` numbers it records the
fine best-approximation constant `λ(α)`, whose discrete part below `3` is
`{√(9m²−4)/m : m a Markov number}` — `√5 < √8 < √(221)/5 < …`.  This is one of the standard
invariants genuinely *finer than* `μ`, and it falls out of the repo's own quadratic forms.

  - the **golden form** `Q(m,k) = m² − mk − k²` — the `P = [[2,1],[1,1]]` spiral invariant,
    discriminant `5` — is the **first Markov form**, value `√5`, the spectrum minimum
    (the golden ratio φ, the worst-approximable number);
  - the **silver form** `x² − 2y²`, discriminant `8`, is the second, value `√8` (`√2`);
  - both fall out of the repo's two `∅`-axiom irrational seeds, and the whole tree is
    generated by Vieta jumping on `x² + y² + z² = NS·xyz`, the atomic coefficient `NS = 3`.

## Narrative

### The golden form is the first Markov form

An indefinite binary quadratic form `Q` of discriminant `D` has Markov value `M(Q) = √D /
inf_{(m,k)≠0} |Q(m,k)|`.  For the golden form `D = 5`.  `golden_anisotropic` proves `Q = 0`
only at the origin, by a **Vieta descent**: writing `m = k + r`, the equation `m² = mk + k²`
becomes `k² = kr + r²` — the *same* form one level down with `r < k` — so a fuel-bounded
structural induction (no `mod 5`, no `omega`) drives `(m,k)` to the origin.  Hence
`inf|Q| = 1` (attained at `(1,0)`, and `Q(F_{n+1},F_n) = ±1` along the Fibonacci orbit),
and `M(Q) = √5` — the **minimum of the Lagrange spectrum**, the constant of the golden ratio
φ = [1;1,1,…], the worst-approximable real.

The bridge is exact: φ's Fibonacci convergents carry the Cassini cross-determinant `W = ±1`
(`ContinuedFractionFloor.cf_det_sq`), and that `W = ±1` floor *is* the golden form taking its
minimum `±1`.  So the spectrum minimum is read directly off the spiral's det-one floor.

### The silver form and the second value

`x² − 2y²` (discriminant `8`) is anisotropic by the repo's `√2` irrationality
(`silver_anisotropic` from `sqrt2_irrational`); with `inf = 1` its value is `√8 = 2√2`, the
second Lagrange value (the constant of `√2 = [1;2,2,…]`).  So the repo's first two indefinite
forms — golden (disc `5`) and silver (disc `8`), already the exceptional-axis seeds — realise
the first two spectrum values.

### The Markov tree and `NS`

The whole spectrum is generated by the **Markov tree**: the triples solving `x² + y² + z² =
3xyz`, where the coefficient `3` is the atomic `NS` (`markov_coefficient_is_NS`), the trace
of the golden matrix `P`.  The two roots `z, z'` of `t² − 3xy·t + (x²+y²)` satisfy
`z + z' = 3xy`, and `markov_vieta` shows the jump `(x,y,z) ↦ (x,y,z')` preserves the equation
— via the clean invariant `x² + y² = z·z'` (`markov_xy`), proved with no subtraction by
cancelling `z²` from `3xyz = z² + z·z'`.  From the root `(1,1,1)` the branch
`(1,1,1) → (1,1,2) → (1,2,5) → (1,5,13) → (2,5,29)` gives the Markov numbers `1,2,5,13,29`,
whose Lagrange values build the spectrum.

The tree's **left spine is the golden recurrence itself**: the odd-index Fibonacci numbers
`fib 1, fib 3, fib 5, fib 7, fib 9 = 1, 2, 5, 13, 34` are exactly the Markov numbers on the
`(1, F_{2n−1}, F_{2n+1})` branch (`markov_fibonacci_branch`), and the Vieta step
`F_{2n−1} + F_{2n+3} = 3·F_{2n+1}` carries one odd-index Fibonacci to the next.  So the
worst-approximable spine of the approximation spectrum is the same Fibonacci/golden recursion
that runs `P = [[2,1],[1,1]]` — φ at the bottom (`√5`), its Fibonacci convergents climbing
the spine.

The **other principal spine is Pell** (`markov_pell_branch`): the odd-index Pell numbers
`pell 1, pell 3, pell 5, pell 7 = 1, 5, 29, 169` are the Markov numbers on the
`(2, P_{2n−1}, P_{2n+1})` branch, with Vieta step `P_{2n−1} + P_{2n+3} = 6·P_{2n+1}` the Pell
recurrence — the `√2`/silver-form side.  So the two recursions that generate the repo's two
quadratic seeds (golden disc 5, silver disc 8) are exactly the two principal branches of the
Markov tree descending from `(1,1,2)`.

These two spines diverge at a **binary fork** (`markov_first_fork`): `(1,2,5)` has two Vieta
children, `(1,5,13)` (Fibonacci/`√5`, `markov_fork_fibonacci`) and `(2,5,29)` (Pell/`√8`,
`markov_fork_pell`), each produced by the `markov_vieta` engine.  The Markov tree is the
Stern-Brocot binary tree on `SL(2,ℤ)` data, its nodes indexing the Markov numbers.  Whether
that indexing is injective is the classical **Markov uniqueness conjecture** (open); its
∅-axiom arithmetic spine — the neighbor congruence `c ∣ a²+b²` and the `√(−1)` encoding — is
developed in [`markov_uniqueness.md`](markov_uniqueness.md).

### Relation to the spiral classification

This is the approximation-spectrum reading of the spiral invariant.  Where `μ` collapses
φ, e, π, √2 all to `2`, the Markov value separates the *quadratic* ones by the discriminant
of their golden/silver/… form — `√5` for φ, `√8` for √2 — read off the same `W`
cross-determinant and `P`-orbit forms that drive `spiral_coordinate_classification.md`.  The
repo keeps, as native `∅`-axiom integer data (form, discriminant, `W`-floor), the fine
approximation constant that `μ` discards.

## Open frontier

The full Lagrange spectrum below `3` is the rest of the Markov tree (`√221/5`, `√1517/13`,
…); each value needs the anisotropy and minimum of the corresponding Markov form, which the
Vieta tree (`markov_vieta`) generates and which the golden/silver descent generalises.  The
classical Markov-value formula `√(9m²−4)/m` and "`√5` = Lagrange minimum" are cited; the
`∅`-axiom content here is the discriminants, the anisotropy descents, and the tree engine.
