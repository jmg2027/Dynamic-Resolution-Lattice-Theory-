# The naturals from the distinguishing's successor-spine

**Status**: Closed (the Peano / ordered-semiring layer and the four-corner rival

## Overview

There are two readings of the distinguishing that each recover ℕ. The
*count-shadow* reading — append on unit-lists, `count` the homomorphism — is
`theory/math/numbersystems/arithmetic_generation.md`. This chapter is the other:
the **successor-spine** reading. The first distinguishing yields two atoms `a, b`;
iterating the single `slash`-successor "point once more with `a`" from the seed `b`
generates a spine of distinct `Raw`s, and that spine **is** ℕ — its Peano structure,
its ordered-commutative-semiring arithmetic, and its recursion are all carried by
`Raw`, with Lean's `Nat` recovered only as the `depth` **readout** (a Lens reading
*out*, never the source). A companion result shows *no rival primitive* (unary,
higher-arity, non-distinct, or relational) generates the same richness.

The **discipline computed on this carrier** — order, divisibility, gcd, coprimality,
well-ordering, exponentiation, primality, the FTA, and the `p`-adic valuation, all generated
over `Nat213` — is the companion chapter
`theory/math/numbertheory/number_theory_over_the_spine.md`.

## Narrative

### The carrier without `Nat`

`IsRawNat : Raw → Prop` is the inductive closure of the seed `b` under the
`slash`-successor `rawSucc r := slashOrSelf Raw.a r` — two constructors, `base`
(`b`) and `step` (`rawSucc`). **No `Nat` appears in the definition.** The naturals
object is `RawNat := { r : Raw // IsRawNat r }`. Its own recursor `IsRawNat.rec` is
natural-number induction: `rawNat_induction` has a closure containing *only*
`IsRawNat`/`IsRawNat.rec` — no `Nat`, `Nat.rec`, `Nat.lt_wfRel`, `Acc.rec`, or
`WellFounded.fix`. The `∃ n, rawTower n = r` (tower-index) description is a *derived
reading*, equivalent by `isRawNat_iff`, used only to bridge to `depth` facts.

### Peano and the depth isomorphism

`zero := ⟨b, base⟩`; `succ x := ⟨rawSucc x.val, x.2.step⟩` — a step *is* one more
distinguishing, not an imported `+1`. The `depth` Lens reads the spine: `toNat x :=
x.val.depth`, with inverse `ofNat n := rawTower n`. `rawSucc_depth` proves
`(rawSucc r).depth = r.depth + 1` directly (the successor fires as `a / r` since
`a ≠ r` for every closure element, `isRawNat_ne_a`), so `toNat_succ` is itself
`Nat`-free. From depth-injectivity: `succ_inj`, `succ_ne_zero` (via the propext-clean
`Nat.succ.inj`/`Nat.noConfusion`), and `toNat`/`ofNat` are a Peano isomorphism
`RawNat ≃ ℕ` (`toNat_ofNat`, `ofNat_toNat`).

### The ordered commutative semiring

`+` and `·` are generated by iterating the spine: `add x y` applies `rawSucc` to `x`
exactly `depth y` times (`isRawNat_addRaw` keeps the result in the closure), and
`mul` iterates `add`. The `depth` Lens is a **semiring homomorphism** onto ℕ
(`toNat_add`, `toNat_mul`) — the load-bearing content — so every commutative-semiring
law (`add_comm`, `add_assoc`, `zero_add`, `add_zero`, `mul_comm`, `mul_assoc`,
`one_mul`, `mul_one`, `zero_mul`, `mul_zero`, `left_distrib`, `right_distrib`)
transports through `toNat_inj` with no re-proof on `Raw`. The order is **additive
reachability** `le x y := ∃ z, add x z = y` (y is x plus finitely many successors),
proved to coincide with the depth order (`le_iff_toNat : le x y ↔ toNat x ≤ toNat y`);
partial-order + totality + additive monotonicity follow. `RawNat` is an ordered
commutative semiring, realised on the distinguishing's spine.
(`mul_assoc`/`right_distrib` route through `NatHelper.mul_assoc`/`add_mul`, since core
`Nat.mul_assoc`/`Nat.add_mul` leak `propext`.)

### The recursion engine grounded in `Raw`'s descent

Beyond the inductive recursor, the Peano induction is also grounded in `Raw`'s proven
well-founded `slash`-peel: `strongRec_isPart` recurses on `IsPart` (`isPart_wf`, 0
`Nat` constants in its cone), and `rec_grounded` supplies the per-step descent with
`tower_ascent_isPart` (each rung is a part of the next). Closure probe: `isPart_wf`
present, `Nat.lt_wfRel`/`Nat.strongRecOn` absent. So the recursion descends on the
distinguishing's own peel, not `Nat`'s order.

### Two readings of one spine

The single spine reads two ways. `depth`/`toNat` reads it *linearly* (`0,1,2,3,…`);
`census x := rawCount (toNat x)` reads its *population* `2,3,5,12,68,…` (Mingu's
recurrence `rawCount n = 2 + C(rawCount (n−1), 2)` — canonical `Raw`s of depth ≤ n).
`census_succ` re-indexes the recurrence by `RawNat`'s own successor; `two_readings`
exhibits both readings of the same object at the depth-2 rung (`2` vs `5`).

### Why the distinguishing is forced (rival exclusion)

`RivalArity` brackets the `(arity, distinctness)` design space and the relational
alternative — four corners, all ∅-axiom:

| rival | why excluded | theorem |
|---|---|---|
| unary (negation-first) | linear growth `< rawCount` | `binary_non_interchangeable_with_unary` |
| ternary-distinct | sterile on the 2-atom seed (`choose3 2 = 0`) | `ternCount_sterile` |
| non-distinct binary | over-generates (self-combinations) | `nondistinct_rival_exceeds` |
| relation-first | `Bool` codomain, ≤ 2 values, no carrier element | `relation_outputs_le_two` |

The capstone `arity_distinctness_forcing` is the squeeze onto binary-distinct = 213.
A relation's generative face *is* `slash` (`Object1 r = (·=r)` is its `Bool`-valued
relational face); functionalising a relation to generate collapses it to
operation-first.

## Open frontier

- **The kernel `inductive` itself.** `Raw`/`IsRawNat` use the kernel's `inductive`
  mechanism to exist, and `Nat` remains the `depth` readout type. These are the
  conceded boundary of "generated vs borrowed" (the distinguishing *is* an inductive
  act); not eliminable, only accounted for.
- **Leg-3 residue.** The four formalized rival classes are excluded, but this is not a
  proof that *no* conceivable primitive (a differently-seeded or exotic one) generates
  equal richness — "suffices by breadth, not proven unique".
