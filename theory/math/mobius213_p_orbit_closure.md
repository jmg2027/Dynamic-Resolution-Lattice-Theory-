# Möbius P-orbit closure and the naturalness boundary

The Möbius matrix `P = [[2,1],[1,1]]` — algebraic encoding of
the atomic signature `(NS, NT, det) = (3, 2, 1)` — admits a
*dynamic = static* reading.  Iterating P (dynamic) produces the
Lucas-Pell trace sequence `L(k) = trace(P^k)` (static).  The
ring `⟨{L(k) : k ∈ ℕ} ∪ {NT, NS, d}⟩_ℤ` is the **P-orbit
closure**, and it is the precise framework-internal naturalness
boundary for P-related integer invariants.

Companion to `mobius_canonical_equivalence.md` (P as cut
equivalence generator); this chapter pursues the orbit of P
itself across tripartite duality, mod-p periods, and the
Lucas-Pell trace generator.

## Overview

Three sharpenings collapse the original "strict atomic-closure"
claim into the deeper P-orbit structure:

1. **Tripartite K_{NT, det, NS}** — the framework name "213"
   reads literally as `NT − det − NS`, a tripartite structure
   complementing the bipartite K_{NS, NT}.  Bipartite captures
   multiplicative atomic structure `NS · NT`; tripartite
   captures additive structure `NS + NT = d`.
2. **p-adic Lens family is general** — `Lib/Math/Padic/ZpSeq p`
   is parameterised by ANY prime p (smoke tests at p = 7, 11
   exist).  Mod-{7, 11, 13, ...} reductions of P are PURE-Lens
   natural via the p-adic family, not just mod-{2, 3, 5}.
3. **P-orbit closure (the master claim)** — the "non-atomic"
   prime `7` appearing in mod-13 and mod-29 periods is
   `L(2) = trace(P²) = 3·3 − 1·2 = 7`.  Every framework-natural
   integer invariant emerges from some finite depth in the
   Lucas-Pell trace orbit.

## Tripartite K_{2,1,3}

Atomic invariants of `K_{NT, det, NS} = K_{2, 1, 3}`:

  · `|V| = NT + det + NS = 6 = NS · NT`
    (additive sum = bipartite multiplicative product)
  · `|E_glue-mediated| = NT · det + det · NS = d = 5`
    — discriminant d is the *graph-theoretic edge count*
    of the canonical glue-mediated tripartite
  · `|E_complete| = d + NS · NT = 11`
  · `|△(K_{2,1,3})| = NT · det · NS = 6 = NS · NT`
    — tripartite triangle count = bipartite edge count via
    the glue mediator

`TripartiteK213.tripartite_master` (PURE) bundles these as a
5-conjunct capstone (vertex/edge/triangle counts + duality +
`d = NT + NS`).

`bipartite_edge_eq_tripartite_triangle` (PURE) is the atomic-
level duality witness: each bipartite edge of K_{NS, NT}
corresponds to a glue-mediated triangle in K_{NT, det, NS}.

The cohomology-level bridge (Massey on K_{3,2}^{(c=2)} as
shadow projection of natural cohomology on K_{2,1,3}) is open
— see `theory/essays/bipartite_tripartite_self_containment.md`
for the dual reading (the self-containment thesis: every
structural element of K_{3,2}^{(c=2)} already carries both
"2" and "3" simultaneously without needing the K_{2,1,3}
extension).

## Lucas-Pell trace orbit

The non-atomic prime `7` appearing in mod-13 and mod-29 periods
is NOT external — it IS the trace of `P²`:

```
L(2) = NS · L(1) − det · L(0) = 3 · 3 − 1 · 2 = 7
```

where `L(k) := trace(P^k)` is the **Lucas-Pell trace sequence**,
generated by `L(0) = NT = 2, L(1) = NS = 3` and the recurrence
`L(k+2) = NS · L(k+1) − det · L(k)`.

Sequence (decide-verified in `POrbitClosure.lean`):

  L(0) = 2 = NT
  L(1) = 3 = NS
  L(2) = 7     ← the "non-atomic" prime, but = trace(P²)
  L(3) = 18
  L(4) = 47
  L(5) = 123
  L(6) = 322
  L(7) = 843
  …

## Why P, not anything else?

`P` is *dynamic = static* in 213:

  · **Dynamic**: P acts as a Möbius transformation
    `x ↦ (2x + 1) / (x + 1)`, generating an orbit by iteration.
  · **Static**: P is a matrix in SL(2, ℤ), characterised by
    atomic invariants `(trace, det) = (NS, 1)`.

The Lucas-Pell trace sequence `L(k)` is *simultaneously*:

  · the trace of P-iteration (dynamic);
  · a sequence of integers (static).

These two readings coincide on P.  Every static integer that
emerges from any P-related construction is some `L(k)` or a
ℤ-combination thereof — by definition of the framework.

The essay `theory/essays/p_orbit_naturalness_boundary.md`
develops this dynamic-static coincidence at the operational
level (depth-0 atomic vs depth-k iteration; the curatorial
choice of "depth ≤ 1" for the 36-species catalog).

## Bipartite × Tripartite × P-orbit cross-structure

Sharpenings 1 and 3 together give a 2D structure:

  · **Bipartite axis**: K_{NS, NT} = multiplicative atomic
  · **Tripartite axis**: K_{NT, det, NS} = additive atomic
  · **P-orbit axis**: L(k) = depth in P-iteration

Conjecture (open): every framework-natural construction is a
*cross-product* of (bipartite/tripartite location) × (P-orbit
depth).  The 36 atomic catalog is the **depth ≤ 1 slice**; the
full p-adic catalog is the **depth → ∞ direct limit**.
