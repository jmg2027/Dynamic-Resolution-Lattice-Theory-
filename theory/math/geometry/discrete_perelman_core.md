## Narrative

### (i) Weighted IBP — the flow IS the gradient flow (`WeightedGreen`)

On an arbitrary finite graph with symmetric weight `w x y = w y x`
(the discretization of `e^{−f}dV`):

- `weighted_green`: `⟨f,g⟩_w = −2·Σ g·L_w f` — with `g := f` this is
  Perelman's `∫ Δf·e^{−f} = ∫ |∇f|²·e^{−f}`, the move that makes the
  `𝓕`-functional's first variation computable.
- `dirichlet_first_variation` + `dirichlet_gradient_identity`:
  `𝓕_w(f+h) = 𝓕_w(f) − 4·Σ h·L_w f + 𝓕_w(h)` — the heat semigroup
  generated by `L_w` **is** the gradient flow of the weighted Dirichlet
  energy.  `flow_rate_sos` gives the descent rate as a sum of squares;
  `wlap_mass_conservation` the measure-preservation.

The engine is `gridSumZ_antisym_zero`: an antisymmetric kernel's double
sum vanishes — Fubini-reflection plus the division-free `S = −S ⟹ S = 0`
(`eq_zero_of_two_mul_eq_zero`).

### (ii) The `𝓦`-Gaussian (`DiscreteGaussian`)

The binomial kernel `u(t,x) = C(t,x)` is the discrete heat fundamental
solution — Pascal's rule *is* the heat step, definitionally, with
δ-initial data.  `gaussian_normalization`: `Σ_x u(t,x) = 2^t` for **all**
`t` — the exact discrete content of the `(4πτ)^{−n/2}` prefactor (the
kernel stays a probability measure along the flow).  `gaussian_mean`:
`2·Σ x·u = t·2^t` (centre `t/2`, the `τ`-scaling).  The De Moivre–Laplace
continuum limit is *not* claimed.

### (iii) Li–Yau as log-concavity (`Binomial` + `DiscreteGaussian`)

`binom_absorption` — `(k+1)·C(n,k+1) + k·C(n,k) = n·C(n,k)`,
subtraction-free, by pure Pascal double induction — yields
`binom_log_concave`: `C(n,k)·C(n,k+2) ≤ C(n,k+1)²`.  Read on the kernel
(`gaussian_li_yau`), spatial log-concavity is the **division-free cleared
form of `Δ log u ≤ 0`** — the Li–Yau gradient estimate with the
`Real213`-division wall sidestepped by cross-multiplication.
`harnack_forward` propagates positivity forward in time.
