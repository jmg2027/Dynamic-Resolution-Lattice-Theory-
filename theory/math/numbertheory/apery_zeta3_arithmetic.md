# Apéry's recurrence and the ζ(3) arithmetic — the WZ nucleus, ∅-axiom

ζ(3) is built in the repo as a constructed fold (`Real213/Zeta3Cut`): the
convergents of the factorial-cleared Apéry recurrence, folded into an `AbCutSeq`.
That fold is *rate-free* as presented — its Casoratian `6·(m!)⁶` overtakes the
denominator growth — so the unconditional modulus needs the **reduced**
presentation `(2·lcm³·aₙ, 2·lcm³·bₙ)`, which carries the rate `28 > 27 = 3³`.
The reduced presentation rests on two classical arithmetic inputs.  This chapter
collects the ∅-axiom discharge of those inputs — and, at its centre, **Apéry's
recurrence for the binomial sums**, the WZ / creative-telescoping identity that is
the nucleus of the whole route.

## 213-native answer

There is **no rational-number primitive** and **no harmonic-sum primitive** here.
The denominator Apéry numbers are an explicit count —
```
B(n) = Σ_k C(n,k)²·C(n+k,k)²          (manifestly a ℕ-sum)
```
— and *that* `B` is shown to satisfy the second-order recurrence the ζ(3)
convergents are generated by.  The "harmonic" content of ζ(3) never appears as a
`1/j³`: every clearing is the count-Lens read of an integer divisibility
(`j³ ∣ lcm³`, `m·C(k,m) ∣ lcm(1..k)`), and the recurrence is a single subtraction-
free polynomial identity in the additive parametrisation `j = k + d`.  No `ℚ`, no
`Int` (Lean's `Int` lemmas carry `propext`); the whole arithmetic lives in `ℕ`.

## The nucleus — Apéry's recurrence (`apery_recurrence`)

> `(j+2)³·B(j+2) + (j+1)³·B(j) = aperyLead(j)·B(j+1)`,
> `aperyLead j = 34j³ + 153j² + 231j + 117`.

This is *the* identity the entire program reduces to: by the factorial-cleared
orbit recurrence (`Zeta3Cut.aperyOrbit`), `(n!)³·B(n)` satisfies the orbit
recurrence iff `B` satisfies the above, so `zeta3Den n = (n!)³·B(n)` follows by
induction — the denominator orbit is identified with the explicit sum.

**The proof has no clean WZ certificate available a priori** — it was *found* by
exact bivariate interpolation and verified, then re-derived bottom-up:

1. **The cleared certificate** `Ĝ(j,k) = −4k⁴(2j+3)(4j²+12j−2k²+3k+8)·C(j+2,k)²·
   C(j+k,k)²` gives the verified telescoping
   `(j+1)²(j+2)²·[(j+2)³a(j+2,k)+(j+1)³a(j,k)−aperyLead(j)a(j+1,k)] = Ĝ(j,k+1)−Ĝ(j,k)`
   for the summand `a(n,k)=C(n,k)²C(n+k,k)²`.
2. **The `W`-factoring.**  The column recurrences `(n+1−k)C(n+1,k)=(n+1)C(n,k)`
   (`colrec`) and `(k+1)C(n,k+1)=(n−k)C(n,k)` (`lowrec`) — proved subtraction-free
   from `choose_mul_factorials` — express each `a(n,k)·(j+1)²(j+2)²` and the `Gmag`
   certificate magnitude as `W·polynomial`, `W = C(j+2,k)²C(j+k,k)²` (`R0/R1/R2`,
   `G1`).
3. **The polynomial core.**  Factoring `W`, the per-`k` identity collapses to one
   polynomial identity (`reduced_wz_identity`); in the additive `j=k+d`
   parametrisation it is **subtraction-free** (`j+2−k=d+2`, etc.) and closes by the
   ∅-axiom `ring_nat`.  The boundary cases `k∈{j+1,j+2}` (where `j=k+d` fails) and
   `k>j+2` (where `W=0`) are handled separately (`redid_eq`, `per_k`).
4. **The telescoped sum.**  Summing `per_k` over `k<j+3`, the `Gmag` terms
   telescope (`sumTo_shift_eq`, boundaries `Gmag(j,0)=Gmag(j,j+3)=0`); the
   `a`-sums extend to `B`; cancelling `(j+1)²(j+2)²>0` gives the recurrence.

The crucial design choice throughout: stay additive in `ℕ`.  The certificate
factor `Q=4j²+12j−2k²+3k+8` is `>0` on the whole summation range `0≤k≤j+2`, so the
cleared identity never needs `Int` or an even/odd split.

## Input I2 — the lcm race (`lcmUpTo_le`)

> `lcm(1..n) ≤ 10^{15⌈n/30⌉} ≈ (√10)ⁿ < 3.236ⁿ = α^{1/3·n}`.

The finitized-Chebyshev chain (NOT Hanson — its margin is too thin and its tail
needs unbounded nested-exponent bookkeeping).  Per prime power, Legendre's
factorial formula `vₚ(n!)=Σ⌊n/p^{j+1}⌋` (`legendre`, ∅-axiom via the increment
route, no Fubini) and the lcm-side count `vₚ(lcm 1..N)=#{f≥1:pᶠ≤N}` (`vp_lcmUpTo`,
via the `floorLog` sandwich) feed the 30-periodic counting lemma `count30`; this
gives `key_divisibility`, the factorial-ratio bound `step3` (`α₃₀=2¹⁴3⁹5⁵`,
`37·α₃₀⁶ ≤ 10⁷⁵`), and the numeral induction `step5`–`main`.

## Input I1 — the divisibility engines (KeyDiv, Heart)

- **KeyDiv** (`keydiv`): `m·C(k,m) ∣ lcm(1..k)` for `1≤m≤k`, witnessed
  *without* `ord_p` by the finite-difference identity `Σⱼ(−1)ʲC(s,j)·Πᵢ≠ⱼ(m+i)=s!`
  (`fd_identity`, encoded as even/odd `ℕ` sums to avoid `Int`).
- **Heart** (`heart`): `m·C(k,m)∣d → m³C(n,m)C(n+m,m) ∣ d³C(n,k)C(n+k,k)`, by
  substituting `d=m·C(k,m)·Q` into the cube and collapsing via the trinomial double
  identity `aperyTrinomial`.
- **Per-term engines** for the integrality assembly: `cube_dvd_lcm_cube`
  (`j³∣lcm³`) and `heart_lcm` (Heart at `d=lcm(1..n)`).
- **The common factor**: `two_lcmCube_dvd_factCube` (`2·lcm³∣(n!)³`, `n≥4`), so
  `c n=(n!)³/(2lcm³)∈ℕ` — the `2` from `2` and `2^{⌊log₂n⌋}` being distinct factors
  `≤n` (`v₂(n!)≥v₂(lcm)+1`).

## Open frontier

The route's remaining half is the **numerator integrality** `(n!)³ ∣ 2·lcm³·
zeta3Num n`.  The numerator Apéry number `A(n)=H₃(n)·B(n)+K(n)` is harmonic
(`H₃(n)=Σ1/j³`, kernel `K`), satisfies the *same* recurrence, and `2lcm³A(n)∈ℤ`.
Unlike the denominator it has **no clean WZ certificate**; the recurrence needs the
explicit Apéry kernel telescoping.  The `H₃` part is already cleared from
`apery_recurrence` (`Zeta3Numerator.harmonic_part_recurrence`); the kernel
inhomogeneous recurrence is the live frontier, tracked in
`research-notes/frontiers/zeta3_wz/`.  Closing it, with the piecewise `(c,p,q)` and
the `htel` margin, makes `zeta3HolonomicReal` unconditional.
