# P-orbit as the naturalness boundary

The Möbius matrix `P = [[2, 1], [1, 1]]` is *dynamic = static*
in 213: P acts as a Möbius transformation (dynamic) and is
characterised by atomic invariants (static).  The Lucas-Pell
trace sequence `L(k) = trace(P^k)` is simultaneously the trace
of P-iteration and a static sequence of integers.  These two
readings coincide, and the ring `⟨{L(k)} ∪ {NT, NS, d}⟩_ℤ` is
the precise framework-internal naturalness boundary for
P-related integer invariants.

## 213-native answer

A framework-natural integer invariant for any P-related
species (mod-p periods of P, Cassini-type identities, Pell
unit cross-products, ...) is one that:

  · admits a PURE Lens realisation (Lib/Math/NumberSystems/Padic/ZpSeq p
    parameterised by any prime); or equivalently
  · belongs to the ring `⟨{L(k) : k ∈ ℕ} ∪ {NT, NS, d}⟩_ℤ`
    (`NaturalnessClosure.lean` + `POrbitClosure.lean`).

These two conditions agree.  The integer `7`, which appears
as a factor of mod-13 period `14` and mod-29 period `7`, is
NOT external: it is `L(2) = trace(P²) = 3·3 − 1·2 = 7`.  Every
mod-p period in the catalog is P-orbit-derivable as a finite
ℤ-combination of `L(k)`s times atomic monomials.

## Derivation

`L(k)` is generated by `L(0) = NT = 2, L(1) = NS = 3` and the
Pell-Lucas recurrence `L(k+2) = NS · L(k+1) − det · L(k)`:

  L(0) = 2 = NT
  L(1) = 3 = NS
  L(2) = 7
  L(3) = 18
  L(4) = 47
  L(5) = 123
  L(6) = 322
  L(7) = 843

The recurrence is the characteristic polynomial of P (Cayley-
Hamilton): `P² = trace(P) · P − det(P) · I`, taking trace gives
`L(k+2) = trace(P) · L(k+1) − det(P) · L(k)`.  Two readings,
same equation:

  · **Dynamic**: iterate P, take trace at each step.
  · **Static**: solve a linear recurrence with characteristic
    roots `{φ², 1/φ²}` (= eigenvalues of P).

Mod-p periods of P then read off via:

| p | period | P-orbit form | depth |
|---|---|---|---|
| 2 | 3 | L(1) | 1 |
| 13 | 14 | NT · L(2) | 2 |
| 17 | 18 | L(3) | 3 |
| 29 | 7 | L(2) | 2 |
| 37 | 38 | L(3) + NT² · d | 3 (additive) |
| 53 | 54 | NS · L(3) | 3 |

Every entry expressible as a ℤ-combination of L(k)'s.  The
naturalness predicate is "exists a finite k such that the
period is P-orbit-derivable at depth ≤ k".

`POrbitClosure.framework_natural_via_p_orbit_closure` (PURE):
the mod-13 and mod-29 cases — the "first real break" of strict
atomic-derivable closure — are P-orbit-derivable at depth 2.

## Dual function

This is not "213 had an over-tight atomic catalog and we
relaxed it".  The original 36-species catalog (G144) was
maximally tight under one curatorial choice: depth ≤ 1 of
the P-orbit.  The relaxation to full P-orbit-derivability is
not a weakening of standards — it is the precise statement
of WHICH standard the depth-1 catalog approximates.

The classical reading would treat the appearance of `7` in
mod-13 period as either:
  · a falsifier of the "atomic-only" claim (mathematician
    Anatole Beuf's mod-7 objection), or
  · a contingent number-theoretic accident.

The P-orbit reading absorbs both: `7` is neither external nor
accidental.  It is the depth-2 trace of P.  The naturalness
boundary moves from "is in `⟨{NT, NS, d}⟩_ℤ`" (depth 0) to
"is in `⟨{L(k)} ∪ {NT, NS, d}⟩_ℤ`" (depth ≤ ω).  The framework
remains tight; the tightness sits one level deeper than
previously stated.

## Cross-frame connections

Three convergent readings of "what does P generate":

  · **Algebraic**: P is the unique 2×2 ℤ-matrix with
    (trace, det) = (NS, 1) and discriminant = NS + NT = d
    (`Mobius213.mobius_213_pell_unit_invariant_forall`).
  · **Dynamical**: P acts on ℚ ∪ {∞} as
    `x ↦ (2x + 1) / (x + 1)`, with attracting fixed point
    φ² = (3 + √5)/2 and repelling 1/φ²
    (`Mobius213.lean` §3).
  · **Combinatorial**: P generates the Stern-Brocot tree via
    mediants of `(0, 1), (1, 0)`; every coprime pair is
    reached uniquely (`Mobius213SternBrocot.lean`).

The Lucas-Pell trace sequence is the IMAGE of all three
readings as a 1-dimensional output: the (a, b) Pseq pairs
project to `a + b` traces under the matrix-trace map.  And
the projection respects the recurrence in every reading.

This convergence is the operational content of "P is the
framework's complete naturalness generator" — three
inequivalent definitions all yielding the same `L(k)` sequence
(and the same closure ring).

## Self-naturalisation refined

The earlier claim (G144 §20.5): 213 is self-naturalising via
PURE-Lens admissibility, matching the atomic catalog.

The refined claim: 213's naturalness predicate = PURE-Lens
admissibility = P-orbit-derivability.  The atomic catalog is
a strictly smaller sub-closure (depth ≤ 1).  The "naturalness
gap" closed by 213 is real; the gap closes at the P-orbit
level, not the atomic level.

This refinement preserves the self-naturalisation thesis
(213 doesn't borrow naturalness from an external standard);
it sharpens the boundary inside which "natural" lives.

## Open frontier

  · **P-orbit closure theorem**: prove that for every prime p,
    `ord(P mod p) ∈ ⟨{L(k)} ∪ {NT, NS, d}⟩_ℤ`.  This is a
    number-theoretic claim about Pell-Lucas sequences mod p
    (related to Lucas's theorem on Lucas sequence periods).
  · **Theory.Atomicity.OrbitForcing**: extend
    Theory.Atomicity with an `OrbitForcing` lemma — atomic
    signature plus the Pell-Lucas recurrence (= P-iteration)
    is forced.
  · **n-prime depth bound**: for primes p, what is the minimum
    L(k) depth?  Is the depth function `p ↦ min{k : ord(P mod p)
    ∈ depth-k ring}` bounded universally, or does it grow with
    p?
  · **Cross-product with bipartite/tripartite axis**:
    conjecture (open) that every framework-natural construction
    is a cross-product of (bipartite/tripartite location) ×
    (P-orbit depth) — see
    `mobius213_p_orbit_closure.md` §"Bipartite × Tripartite ×
    P-orbit cross-structure".
