# Stern-Brocot as the universal lattice for bipartite multigraphs

Every bipartite multigraph `K_{NS, NT}^{(c)}` carries a unique
position in a 3-axis lattice — (Stern-Brocot path, gcd-scale,
multiplicity).  The Stern-Brocot tree is the *universal sample
space*: every coprime pair is the position of exactly one
graph, and the mediant operation `(a, b) ⊕ (c, d) = (a+c, b+d)`
governs how nearby graphs cohere.

## 213-native answer

The Möbius matrix `P = [[2, 1], [1, 1]]` generates the
Stern-Brocot tree via mediants of the seeds `(0, 1)` and
`(1, 0)`.  Every coprime pair appears uniquely at finite depth
(Stern-Brocot node theorem;
`Mobius213SternBrocot.SternBrocotReachable`).

Every `K_{NS, NT}^{(c)}` factors through the lattice:

  · (NS/gcd, NT/gcd) — Stern-Brocot path of the coprime
    reduction (unique mediant path);
  · gcd(NS, NT) — scale factor along the diagonal `{(N, N)}`;
  · c — multiplicity layer count (orthogonal axis).

The atomic anchor `(NS, NT) = (3, 2)` sits at depth-2 of the
`Pseq seedZero` thin chain — `(3, 2) = Pseq seedZero 2`,
mediant of (2, 1) and (1, 1).  The forced atomicity from
`Theory.Atomicity.PairForcing` lands EXACTLY on a Stern-Brocot
orbit point.

## Dual function

This is not "Stern-Brocot tree happens to enumerate the graphs we
care about".  The Stern-Brocot tree is generated by the SAME
matrix `P` that algebraically encodes the atomic signature
(trace = NS, det = 1, disc = d).  So the lattice is
*self-described* by the atomicity:

  · The tree's generator is P.
  · P's matrix invariants are (NS, NT, det).
  · The atomic signature (3, 2) is a depth-2 orbit point of P.

The lattice and its anchor point are produced by the same
algebraic object.  "Bipartite multigraph K_{NS, NT}" is then
not a free combinatorial choice — it is a coordinate in the
Möbius P lattice, with the (3, 2) anchor distinguished by being
the depth-2 image of the seed.

The classical reading would treat "K_{NS, NT} for various NS, NT"
as separate combinatorial objects each requiring fresh
cohomology computation.  The Stern-Brocot reading reveals these
graphs as samples of ONE lattice — and cohomological proofs
respect the lattice structure:
`KernelConstancyUniversal.universal_kernel_close` (PURE) shows
`b₀ = 1` (connectedness) uniformly across the whole
`K_{NS, NT}^{(c)}` family from a single parametric statement.
Structural facts transport along the Stern-Brocot tree.

  · **Order-theoretic**: the Stern-Brocot tree enumerates ℚ≥0
    uniquely via mediants — every rational has one path.
  · **Algebraic**: SL₂(ℤ) acts on (a, b) ∈ ℕ² preserving the

Stern-Brocot path × scale axes place each graph; `c` is the
multiplicity layer count, a derived presentation parameter that
