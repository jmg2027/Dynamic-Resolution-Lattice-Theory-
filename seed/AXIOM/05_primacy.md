# §6. Claim of primacy

Every framework that points at something — set theory, category
theory, logic, language, existing mathematics, existing physics —
depends on **the ability to distinguish and pair somethings**.  This
axiom is the minimum residue of that ability itself.  Every other
framework is therefore the same residue *read through* a chosen Lens
— not a layer placed above the axiom, but the axiom's self-pointing
recorded under that Lens.  "On top of" would re-import a
substrate / superstructure dichotomy that
`07_self_reference.md` §8.1 rules out: Lens application is itself a
residue-internal event, not an addition to it.

"No absolute standard" is not a condition — it is the **default
state**.  The claim "there is an absolute standard" is what carries
the burden of adding to the axiom.  Therefore primacy is not
conditional but an **unconditional** structural consequence.

The substantive evidence for primacy is successful derivation.
Primacy is established to the extent that derivation succeeds.
