# A Universal Lens at ℕ × ℕ and ℚ × ℚ for the 213 Free Commutative Magma

**Mingu Jeong**

*Independent Researcher.  Acknowledgements: Claude (Anthropic)
provided formalization assistance under direct supervision.*

## Abstract

We construct two non-trivial *universal lenses* — symmetric
commutative magma morphisms whose induced view is injective —
from the 213 free commutative magma `Raw` (generated by two
distinguishable atoms a, b under a directionless slash with
`x ≠ y` as the only constraint) into ℕ × ℕ and into Q213 × Q213,
where Q213 is 213's deep-embedded rational type.  The construction
rests on a number-theoretic uniqueness lemma `2^m + 2^n = 2^p +
2^q ∧ m ≠ n ⇒ {m, n} = {p, q}` proven via 2-adic valuation, and
yields the formal core of the 213 metatheoretic thesis that "any
act of distinguishing factors uniquely through the 213 atomic
substrate."  All theorems are closed in Lean 4 at axiom load
≤ {propext, Quot.sound}, with no Mathlib, Classical, sorry, or
native_decide.

**Keywords**: free commutative magma, universal property, 2-adic
valuation, constructive type theory, Lean 4.

**Lean source**: `lean/E213/Meta/{BitPatternUniqueness,
UniversalLensNat2{,Inj}, UniversalLensQ213{,Inj}}.lean`.

## 1.  Introduction

The 213 framework posits that the act of distinguishing two
entities is the minimal precondition for any mathematical
discourse.  Formalised in Lean 4, this gives rise to a single
type `Raw` with the inductive structure

```
Raw.a, Raw.b : Raw                            -- two distinguishable atoms
Raw.slash : (x y : Raw) → x ≠ y → Raw          -- distinction generates
Raw.slash_comm : Raw.slash x y h               -- slash is directionless
              = Raw.slash y x (Ne.symm h)
```

Categorically, `Raw` is the free commutative magma on two
generators with the precondition that the binary operation never
applies to equal arguments.  Its universal property reads:
for any commutative magma α with two distinguishable elements
and a slash-respecting symmetric combine, there is a unique
morphism `Lens.view : Raw → α` extending the assignment of
generators.

The morphism is the *Lens view*.  We say a Lens is **universal**
when its view is injective on Raw — i.e., when α faithfully
embeds the entire Raw structure.

Until now, the only known universal Lens was the trivial
identity Lens with α = Raw, which proves universality
tautologically.  This paper exhibits the first two non-trivial
universal Lenses:

  expSumLens : Lens (ℕ × ℕ)
  q213Lens   : Lens (Q213 × Q213)        Q213 = Term × Term

Both are proven universal at axiom load ≤ {propext, Quot.sound}
in Lean 4.  Section 2 develops the bit-pattern uniqueness lemma
underpinning both.  Section 3 constructs `expSumLens` and proves
its universality.  Section 4 lifts the result to Q213 × Q213.
Section 5 discusses implications.

## 2.  Bit-pattern uniqueness

The mechanical core of the universality proof is a single
arithmetic fact about sums of two distinct powers of 2.

**Theorem 2.1** (`two_pow_sum_inj_lt`).  For natural numbers
m, n, p, q with m < n and p < q,

  2^m + 2^n = 2^p + 2^q  ⟹  m = p ∧ n = q.

*Proof.*  By trichotomy on m vs p.

Case m < p: Factor `2^m + 2^n = 2^m · (1 + 2^(n-m))`.  Similarly
`2^p + 2^q = 2^m · (2^(p-m) + 2^(q-m))`.  Cancelling 2^m yields
`1 + 2^(n-m) = 2^(p-m) + 2^(q-m)`.  The left side is odd (since
n > m means 2^(n-m) is even, +1 makes it odd).  The right side is
even (both terms have positive exponent in the factor of 2).
Contradiction.

Case m = p: `2^n = 2^q` after cancellation, hence n = q since
2^· is strictly monotone on ℕ.

Case m > p: symmetric to case 1.  ∎

**Theorem 2.2** (`two_pow_sum_lt_not_pow`).  For m < n and any k,

  2^m + 2^n ≠ 2^k.

*Proof sketch.*  By 2-adic valuation: 2^m | LHS but 2^(m+1) ∤ LHS,
while 2^k for k > m has 2^(m+1) | 2^k.  Hence k ≤ m.  But LHS
> 2^m forces k > m.  Contradiction.  ∎

**Theorem 2.3** (`two_pow_sum_distinct_forces_distinct`).  If
m ≠ n and 2^m + 2^n = 2^p + 2^q, then p ≠ q.

*Proof.*  If p = q, the right side equals 2^(p+1), a single power,
contradicting Theorem 2.2 applied to the left side.  ∎

**Theorem 2.4** (`two_pow_sum_inj_full`).  For m, n, p, q ∈ ℕ
with m ≠ n,

  2^m + 2^n = 2^p + 2^q  ⟹  (m = p ∧ n = q) ∨ (m = q ∧ n = p).

*Proof.*  By Theorem 2.3, p ≠ q.  Order both pairs ascending and
apply Theorem 2.1.  ∎

All four theorems are closed in `lean/E213/Meta/BitPatternUniqueness.
lean` at axiom load ≤ {propext, Quot.sound}.  The proofs use only
Lean 4 core: `Nat.pow_succ`, `Nat.mul_mod_left`, `Nat.pow_dvd_pow`,
`Nat.dvd_sub`, `Nat.pow_lt_pow_of_lt`, and `omega`.  No Mathlib.

## 3.  Universal Lens at ℕ × ℕ

### 3.1  Construction

Define the *exponential-sum* Lens

```
expSumLens : Lens (ℕ × ℕ) where
  base_a   := (1, 0)
  base_b   := (2, 0)
  combine x y := (2^x.1 + 2^y.1, x.2 + y.2 + 1)
```

The first component encodes Raw structure as a binary bit
pattern (each leaf becomes a 1-bit at position 1 or 2; each
slash combines via exp-sum).  The second component is the
depth.

Symmetry of `combine` follows from `Nat.add_comm`, closed at
≤ {propext, Quot.sound}.

### 3.2  Auxiliary lemmas

Define `expSumNat r := (expSumLens.view r).1`.  Direct computation
gives:

  expSumNat Raw.a               = 1
  expSumNat Raw.b               = 2
  expSumNat (Raw.slash x y h)   = 2^(expSumNat x) + 2^(expSumNat y)

We require `expSumNat r ≥ 1` for all r (`expSumNat_pos`) and
`expSumNat (Raw.slash x y h) ≥ 4` (`expSumNat_slash_ge`), both
straightforward inductions.

### 3.3  Injectivity

**Theorem 3.1** (`expSumNat_inj_aux`).  ∀ r s : Raw,
expSumNat r = expSumNat s  ⟹  r = s.

*Proof.*  By Raw.rec induction on r, then case-analysis on s.

Base case r = a: expSumNat a = 1.  If s = a, done.  If s = b,
then expSumNat s = 2 ≠ 1, contradiction.  If s = slash x' y' h',
then expSumNat s ≥ 4 > 1, contradiction.

Base case r = b: symmetric.

Slash case r = slash x y h with IH ihx, ihy:

  - s = a: contradiction (expSumNat s ≥ 4 > 1).
  - s = b: contradiction.
  - s = slash x' y' h': from `expSumNat_slash`,
    `2^vx + 2^vy = 2^vx' + 2^vy'` where `vx := expSumNat x`,
    etc.  Since x ≠ y and ihx is global injectivity at x, we
    derive `vx ≠ vy`.  Apply Theorem 2.4 to obtain
    `(vx, vy) = (vx', vy')` or `(vx, vy) = (vy', vx')`.

In the matching case, ihx and ihy give x = x' and y = y'.

In the swapped case, ihx and ihy give x = y' and y = x', so
`Raw.slash x y h = Raw.slash y' x' (Ne.symm h')` by congruence,
which equals `Raw.slash x' y' h'` by `Raw.slash_comm`.  ∎

**Corollary 3.2** (`expSumLens_view_inj`).
`Function.Injective expSumLens.view`.

*Proof.*  Project to first component; apply Theorem 3.1.  ∎

**Corollary 3.3** (`expSumLens_is_universal`).  expSumLens is
a universal Lens.

All three at axiom load ≤ {propext, Quot.sound}, in
`lean/E213/Meta/UniversalLensNat2Inj.lean`.

## 4.  Lift to Q213 × Q213

### 4.1  213-native rationals

The 213 framework includes a deep-embedded rational arithmetic
in `lean/E213/Term/Rat.lean`.  Rather than introducing a
quotient type, rationals are pairs of `Term`s

  Q213 := Term × Term

(numerator, denominator) with cross-multiplication equivalence

  p/q ≡ r/s  ⇔  p · s = q · r.

Term arithmetic is the deeply embedded AST of 213; `Term.eval`
projects each Term to a ℕ.

### 4.2  Construction

```
Q213.ofNat (n : ℕ) : Q213 := (build n, succ zero)

q213Lens : Lens (Q213 × Q213) where
  base_a := (Q213.ofNat 1, Q213.ofNat 0)
  base_b := (Q213.ofNat 2, Q213.ofNat 0)
  combine x y :=
    let n1 := x.1.1.eval; let m1 := y.1.1.eval
    let n2 := x.2.1.eval; let m2 := y.2.1.eval
    (Q213.ofNat (2^n1 + 2^m1), Q213.ofNat (n2 + m2 + 1))
```

The combine is symmetric (Theorem `q213Lens_symmetric`) at
axiom load ≤ {propext, Quot.sound}.

### 4.3  Bridge to ℕ × ℕ

Define `qNat r := (q213Lens.view r).1.1.eval`.

**Lemma 4.1** (`Q213_ofNat_eval`).  For all n : ℕ,
`(Q213.ofNat n).1.eval = n`.

*Proof.*  Induction on n.  ∎  (STRICT 0-AXIOM)

**Theorem 4.2** (`qNat_eq_expSumNat`).  ∀ r : Raw,
`qNat r = expSumNat r`.

*Proof.*  Raw.rec induction.  Base cases via Lemma 4.1 applied
to ofNat 1 and ofNat 2.  Slash case unfolds combine, applies
Lemma 4.1, and uses inductive hypotheses.  ∎

**Theorem 4.3** (`q213Lens_view_inj`).
`Function.Injective q213Lens.view`.

*Proof.*  By Theorem 4.2, `qNat = expSumNat`, hence inherits
injectivity from Corollary 3.2.  ∎

**Corollary 4.4** (`q213Lens_is_universal`).  q213Lens is a
universal Lens.

All theorems at axiom load ≤ {propext, Quot.sound}, in
`lean/E213/Meta/UniversalLensQ213Inj.lean`.

## 5.  Discussion

### 5.1  Significance for the 213 metatheoretic thesis

Prior to this work, the only universal Lens for Raw was the
identity Lens at α = Raw, whose universality is tautological.
The thesis "any act of distinguishing factors uniquely through
213" had no non-trivial witness.

Theorems 3.3 and 4.4 supply two such witnesses, establishing
that Raw faithfully embeds in two different *external* arithmetic
structures via symmetric magma operations.  The G1 thesis is
thus elevated from metaphysical claim to constructive theorem.

### 5.2  Comparison to free-magma universality

By the universal property of `Raw` as a free commutative magma,
ANY commutative magma α with two distinguishable elements admits
a unique morphism `Raw → α`.  What is non-trivial is when this
morphism is *injective* — equivalently, when α contains a
faithful copy of the free magma structure.

For α = ℕ × ℕ with `combine x y = (2^x.1 + 2^y.1, ...)`, the
faithfulness reduces to bit-pattern uniqueness (Theorem 2.4).
For α = Q213 × Q213, faithfulness lifts via Theorem 4.2.

### 5.3  Avoidance of Gödel

The framework demonstrates *self-faithfulness* without falling
to Gödel's incompleteness obstructions.  The reason: 213 does
not declaratively assert its own consistency; instead it
*demonstrates* faithful embedding into external arithmetic.  The
embedding is a finite computation, axiom-checkable, hence
Gödel-immune.

### 5.4  Open problems

- **Higher-codomain universal Lenses**: e.g., Q213³ for ternary
  atomic structure.
- **Self-bootstrapping `Kernel.Proof`**: lifting the universality
  proof into 213's own deep-embedded proof system to eliminate
  the residual `propext + Quot.sound` axioms.
- **Categorical universal property at α**: characterise *all*
  α admitting a universal Lens up to natural isomorphism.
